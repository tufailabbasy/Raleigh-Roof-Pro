Roofing Materials Guide

Understanding your options is the first step to choosing the right roof. Explore the materials we install and find the perfect fit for your home and budget.

Choosing the Right Roofing Material

Your choice of roofing material affects your home's appearance, energy efficiency, maintenance requirements, and long-term costs. The right material depends on your architectural style, budget, climate considerations, and how long you plan to stay in your home. At Raleigh Roof Pro, we install a full range of roofing materials and help you understand the pros and cons of each option.

Asphalt Shingles

Asphalt shingles are the most populaRaleigh Roof Pro material in America, covering more than 80% of residential roofs. They offer an excellent balance of affordability, performance, and aesthetics.

3-Tab Shingles

3-tab shingles are the most economical asphalt option. They feature a flat appearance with uniform tabs creating a geometric look. While less durable than architectural shingles, they provide reliable protection and are often used in rental properties or budget-conscious projects. Typical lifespan is 15-20 years.

Architectural Shingles (Dimensional)

architectural shingles, also called dimensional or laminated shingles, are the most popular choice for Raleigh homeowners. Multiple layers create a dimensional, textured appearance that mimics wood shake or slate from a distance. They're significantly more durable than 3-tab shingles, with lifespans of 25-30 years and better wind resistance.

Premium/Designer Shingles

Premium shingles offer the highest quality in asphalt roofing. They feature enhanced aesthetics including luxury slate or shake appearances, superior durability with lifespans of 30+ years, excellent wind and impact resistance, and the strongest warranty coverage. We install premium shingle lines from GAF, Owens Corning, and CertainTeed.

Metal Roofing

Metal roofing has surged in popularity as homeowners discover its exceptional longevity, energy efficiency, and modern aesthetics. What was once associated only with commercial or agricultural buildings is now a premium choice for homes.

Standing Seam Metal

Standing seam systems feature raised vertical seams that interlock, creating a clean, contemporary look. Concealed fasteners eliminate potential leak points and allow for thermal movement. Available in steel, aluminum, copper, and zinc, standing seam roofs can last 50-70 years with minimal maintenance. Learn more about metal roofing.

Metal Shingles

Metal shingles provide the durability of metal with traditional shingle appearances. Stamped to resemble wood shake, slate, or tile, they're ideal for neighborhoods with aesthetic requirements that preclude standing seam profiles. Lifespan is typically 40-50 years.

Stone-Coated Steel

Stone-coated steel shingles feature a steel base covered with stone granules, combining metal durability with the appearance of traditional roofing materials. They offer excellent impact and fire resistance.

Slate Roofing

Natural slate is the gold standard in roofing materials. Genuine slate roofs can last 100+ years, making them a true lifetime roof. Slate is fireproof, environmentally friendly, and offers unmatched beauty that only improves with age.

The premium cost of slate (both material and specialized installation) makes it most common on high-end custom homes and historic properties. For homeowners who love the slate look at a lower price point, synthetic slate products offer similar aesthetics with easier installation.

Tile Roofing

Clay and concrete tiles are popular in Spanish, Mediterranean, and Southwestern architectural styles. Tiles offer excellent durability (50-100 years), fire resistance, and distinctive curb appeal. Their weight requires structural verification, and proper installation is critical for long-term performance.
