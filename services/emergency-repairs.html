When Every Minute Counts, Call Raleigh Roof Pro

Roof emergencies don't wait for convenient business hours. When a sudden storm tears off shingles, a tree limb crashes through your roof, or you discover water pouring into your home, you need immediate help from professionals who can stop the damage and protect your property. R Roofing provides 24/7 emergency roof repair services throughout Raleigh and the Triangle area.

Our emergency response team understands that roof damage leads to cascading problems inside your home. Water intrusion causes ceiling damage, destroys insulation, promotes mold growth, and can damage valuable possessions and electronics. The faster we can secure your roof, the less interior damage you'll face and the lower your overall repair costs will be.

We respond quickly because we know what's at stake. When you call our emergency line, a real person answers, not a voicemail system. We dispatch crews equipped with tarps, emergency repair materials, and the tools needed to stabilize your roof until permanent repairs can be completed.

Our Emergency Roof Services

Emergency Roof Tarping

Emergency tarping is the critical first step in preventing further damage after a roof breach. Our crews arrive with heavy-duty tarps sized for residential and commercial applications, weighted systems to secure tarps in high winds, and the expertise to cover damaged areas completely.

Professional tarping isn't just throwing a tarp over a hole. We ensure complete coverage of exposed areas with proper overlap and secure fastening that will hold through subsequent weather events until permanent repairs are scheduled. Proper tarping can protect your home for weeks or even months while insurance claims are processed and materials are ordered.

Temporary Leak Sealing

For smaller breaches and active leaks, we can often apply temporary sealants, emergency flashing repairs, or targeted patching to stop water intrusion immediately. These temporary measures buy time for proper permanent repairs while keeping your home dry and preventing continued water damage.

Tree and Debris Removal

Fallen trees and large debris pose ongoing risks after initial impact. Shifting weight can cause additional structural damage, and unstable debris prevents accurate damage assessment. We work with tree removal specialists to safely remove debris from your roof, then assess and address the underlying roof damage.

Structural Assessment

Beyond the obvious damage visible from ground level, severe roof damage can compromise underlying structural elements. Our emergency team assesses rafters, decking, and support structures to identify hidden damage before it causes additional problems. We document all findings for your insurance claim.

Board-Up Services

When storm damage creates openings in walls, skylights, or exposed areas, we provide board-up services to secure your property against weather, animals, and unauthorized entry. Securing your home is an essential step in the emergency response process.

Common Roof Emergencies We Handle

Storm Damage

North Carolina experiences severe weather throughout the year, including hurricanes and tropical storms, severe thunderstorms with damaging winds, hail storms that destroy shingles, and tornadoes and microbursts. Post-storm, we respond rapidly to tarping needs across the region, prioritizing the most severe damage while working to help all affected homeowners as quickly as possible.

Sudden Roof Leaks

Sometimes roof failures develop gradually until a critical point when water suddenly appears inside your home. These sudden leaks often occur during heavy rains that overwhelm compromised areas. We locate leak sources (which aren't always directly above visible water), apply temporary fixes to stop active leaking, and schedule permanent repairs.

Fallen Trees and Limbs

Large trees and heavy limbs can cause catastrophic roof damage, even penetrating through to interior rooms. These situations require careful debris removal to prevent additional damage, followed by immediate tarping and stabilization. We coordinate with tree services when needed to ensure safe, complete debris removal.

Fire Damage

After a fire, even one contained to a small area, roof damage creates vulnerability to water and weather. We provide emergency covering services to protect fire-damaged structures while restoration planning proceeds.

Wind Damage

High winds can peel back shingles, lift roofing materials, and expose underlayment and decking to water intrusion. Wind damage may not be immediately obvious from ground level but can cause significant problems during subsequent rain events.

What to Do in a Roof Emergency

  1. Ensure Safety First: If a tree has fallen into your home or you see structural damage, evacuate if necessary and call 911 if there are injuries or fire hazards.
  2. Move Valuables: If safe to do so, move furniture and valuables away from areas where water is entering.
  3. Contain Water: Place buckets under active leaks and towels around water to prevent spreading.
  4. Document the Damage: Take photos and videos of damage from safe vantage points for insurance purposes.
  5. Call Raleigh Roof Pro: Reach our emergency line at (919) 739-4051 for immediate assistance.
  6. Contact Your Insurance: Report the damage to your insurance company to begin the claims process.

Preventative Maintenance Prevents Emergencies

While we're always ready to respond to emergencies, the best strategy is preventing them through regular maintenance. Annual roof inspections identify vulnerable areas before they fail during storms. Proactive repairs address small issues before they become emergencies. Regular maintenance extends roof life and reduces lifetime repair costs.

Frequently Asked Questions

How quickly can you respond to a roof emergency?

During normal conditions, we typically respond within 2-4 hours. After major storm events affecting many homes, we prioritize by severity and work through all calls as quickly as possible, usually reaching all emergencies within 24 hours.

How much does emergency tarping cost?

Emergency tarping costs vary based on roof accessibility, damaged area size, and materials required. Many homeowner's insurance policies cover emergency tarping as part of claim mitigation. We provide documentation for insurance reimbursement.

Will my insurance cover emergency repairs?

Most homeowner's insurance policies cover storm damage, fallen trees (depending on source), and resulting emergency measures. We work with all major insurance companies and help document claims properly. Your policy's deductible and coverage limits apply.

Should I try to tarp my own roof?

We strongly advise against DIY roof tarping. Roof surfaces are slippery when wet, damage may be more extensive than visible from ground level, and improperly secured tarps can blow off or cause additional damage. Professional tarping is safer and more effective.
