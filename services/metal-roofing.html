Premium Metal Roofing for Raleigh Homes

Metal roofing has evolved dramatically from the corrugated barn roofs of decades past. Today's metal roofing systems offer stunning aesthetics in a wide range of styles that complement any architectural design, from modern farmhouse to traditional colonial. Combined with unmatched longevity, energy efficiency, and storm resistance, metal roofing represents one of the smartest long-term investments you can make in your home.

In the Raleigh and Triangle area, metal roofs perform exceptionally well against our regional weather challenges. They shed snow and ice quickly, resist wind speeds up to 140 mph (critical during hurricane season), and reflect solar heat to reduce cooling costs during hot Carolina summers. With a lifespan of 50-70 years, many homeowners who install metal roofing never need to replace their roof again.

Raleigh Roof Pro has extensive experience installing premium metal roofing systems throughout Wake County and the surrounding region. Our certified installers are trained in proper metal roof installation techniques that ensure your investment performs as promised for decades to come.

Types of Metal Roofing We Install

Standing Seam Metal Roofing

Standing seam metal roofing is the premium choice for homeowners seeking the ultimate in durability and contemporary aesthetics. The distinctive raised seams run vertically from ridge to eave, creating clean lines that complement modern and farmhouse-style homes beautifully.

Standing seam panels interlock at raised seams, with concealed fasteners that eliminate potential leak points and allow for thermal expansion and contraction. This design results in a watertight roof that performs exceptionally well in high-wind regions. Standing seam roofs are available in steel, aluminum, copper, and zinc, with dozens of color options including factory-applied finishes that resist fading for 30+ years.

While standing seam represents a higher upfront investment than shingles, many homeowners find the 50-70 year lifespan, minimal maintenance requirements, and energy savings make it the most cost-effective choice over time.

Metal Shingles

Metal shingles offer the best of both worlds: the proven durability of metal with the traditional appearance of shake, slate, or tile roofing. Advanced stamping and finishing techniques create remarkably realistic textures that are nearly indistinguishable from natural materials from street level.

Metal shingles are ideal for homeowners in neighborhoods with architectural guidelines that may not permit traditional standing seam profiles. They install similarly to asphalt shingles, making them a more affordable metal option while still delivering 40-50 year lifespans and superior storm resistance.

Corrugated and Ribbed Metal Panels

For agricultural buildings, workshops, barns, and utility structures, corrugated and ribbed metal panels provide economical, durable roofing solutions. While less common on primary residences, these panels are increasingly popular on pool houses, detached garages, and modern industrial-style homes where the utilitarian aesthetic is desired.

Benefits of Metal Roofing

Exceptional Longevity

Metal roofs last 2-3 times longer than asphalt shingles. While a typical asphalt roof lasts 15-25 years and may need replacement during your time in the home, a metal roof installed today could easily last until 2075 or beyond. Many metal roof manufacturers offer 50-year warranties, demonstrating confidence in their products' durability.

Superior Storm Resistance

Metal roofing excels in severe weather conditions common to North Carolina. Standing seam panels rated for 140+ mph wind speeds can withstand even Category 4 hurricane-force winds. Metal roofs also resist hail damage better than asphalt, with many products carrying Class 4 impact ratings (the highest available). After storm events, metal roofs typically require far less repair work than traditional roofing materials.

Energy Efficiency

Metal roofs with reflective coatings can reduce cooling costs by 20-25% compared to standard asphalt shingles. The reflective pigments in cool metal roofing bounce solar radiation back into the atmosphere rather than absorbing it into your attic space. This is especially valuable in Raleigh's hot, humid summers when air conditioning represents a significant portion of energy costs.

Fire Resistance

Metal roofs will not ignite from sparks or embers, providing superior protection in areas where wildfires or house fires are concerns. This fire resistance may also qualify you for insurance premium discounts.

Environmental Benefits

Metal roofing is one of the most environmentally responsible roofing choices. Metal roofs contain 25-95% recycled content and are 100% recyclable at the end of their lifespan (unlike asphalt shingles, which often end up in landfills). Low Maintenance

Unlike wood, slate, or tile roofs that may require periodic treatments or repairs, metal roofs need very little maintenance. Annual visual inspections and occasional debris removal are typically all that's required to keep your metal roof performing optimally for decades.

Metal Roof Colors and Finishes

Modern metal roofing is available in virtually any color you can imagine, from classic earth tones to bold contemporary hues. Premium factory-applied finishes use advanced paint technologies that resist fading, chalking, and chipping far longer than field-applied coatings.

Popular color choices for Raleigh homes include charcoal and slate gray for modern aesthetics, bronze and aged copper for traditional looks, forest green for wooded settings, and classic black for dramatic contrast. Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a metal roof cost in Raleigh?

Metal roofing costs vary by material and style. Standing seam steel typically ranges from $10-18 per square foot installed, while metal shingles range from $8-14 per square foot. A 2,000 square foot roof might cost $16,000-$36,000 depending on complexity and material choice. While higher than asphalt initially, the 50+ year lifespan often makes metal more economical over time.

Are metal roofs noisy in the rain?

No. This is one of the most common misconceptions about metal roofing. When properly installed over solid decking with standard underlayment and attic insulation, metal roofs are no louder than asphalt shingles during rain. The insulation and decking absorb and dampen sound effectively.

Will a metal roof attract lightning?

No. Metal roofs do not attract lightning. Lightning strikes the highest point in an area regardless of material. If lightning did strike, a metal roof would actually be safer than other materials because metal doesn't ignite, and it disperses electrical charge safely.

Can you install solar panels on a metal roof?

Yes! Metal roofs are actually ideal for solar installations. Standing seam roofs allow for non-penetrating mounting systems that clamp directly to the seams, preserving warranty coverage and eliminating roof penetrations. The long lifespan of metal also means you won't need to remove panels for roof replacement.

Can metal roofing be installed over shingles?

In some cases, yes. Metal shingles and some panel systems can be installed over existing asphalt shingles if the deck is in good condition, local codes permit it, and there's only one existing layer. This can reduce installation costs, but we recommend full tear-off for best long-term performance.
