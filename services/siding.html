Expert Siding Services for Raleigh Homeowners

Your home's siding does more than create curb appeal—it serves as a critical protective barrier against water, wind, pests, and temperature extremes. Quality siding installation keeps your home energy-efficient, prevents moisture damage, and adds lasting value to your property. At R Roofing, we provide comprehensive siding services including new installation, replacement, and repair throughout Raleigh and the Triangle area.

North Carolina's climate presents unique challenges for exterior materials. Hot, humid summers can warp poor-quality siding, while winter ice and occasional severe storms can cause impact damage. Our team has years of experience selecting and installing siding products specifically suited to perform in Central North Carolina's variable weather conditions.

Whether you're updating outdated siding, repairing storm damage, or building a new home, we help you choose the right siding material for your budget, aesthetic preferences, and maintenance expectations. From affordable vinyl to premium fiber cement, we install products from leading manufacturers with industry-leading warranties.

Siding Materials We Install

Vinyl Siding

Vinyl remains the most popular siding choice in America for good reason. Modern vinyl siding offers excellent durability, low maintenance, fade resistance, and an extensive selection of colors and profiles. Today's vinyl siding looks remarkably similar to wood clapboard but requires no painting, staining, or scraping.

Vinyl siding is also one of the most affordable options, making it ideal for homeowners who want maximum impact for their renovation budget. Premium vinyl products feature thicker construction for improved durability and can last 30-40 years with minimal maintenance. We install vinyl siding from trusted manufacturers including CertainTeed, James Hardie, and Alside.

Fiber Cement Siding (James Hardie)

Fiber cement siding represents the gold standard for homeowners who want the beautiful appearance of wood with superior durability and minimal maintenance. Made from cement, sand, and cellulose fibers, fiber cement siding resists rot, termites, fire, and impact damage far better than wood or vinyl.

James Hardie is the leading fiber cement brand, and their HardiePlank siding is engineered specifically for different climate zones. The ColorPlus Technology features factory-applied finishes that resist fading, cracking, and peeling far longer than field-applied paint. Fiber cement siding typically lasts 50+ years and comes with substantial manufacturer warranties.

Wood Siding

For homeowners who prefer authentic natural materials, wood siding offers timeless beauty and character that no synthetic product can perfectly replicate. Cedar and redwood are naturally rot-resistant species that perform well in humid climates. Wood siding can be painted or stained in virtually any color and can be refinished multiple times throughout its lifespan.

Wood siding does require more maintenance than vinyl or fiber cement, including periodic painting or staining every 5-7 years. However, many historic homes and custom builds feature wood siding for its authentic appearance and ability to be repaired rather than replaced when damage occurs.

Engineered Wood Siding

Engineered wood products like LP SmartSide offer the appearance of real wood with improved durability and lower maintenance requirements. These products are made from wood fibers bonded with resins and treated for moisture and pest resistance. Engineered wood costs less than natural wood but more than vinyl, occupying a middle ground in price and performance.

Our Siding Services

Complete Siding Replacement

When your existing siding has reached the end of its lifespan, or you simply want a completely new look, our full siding replacement service transforms your home's exterior. The process includes removal of old siding, inspection of underlying sheathing and housewrap, repairs to any damaged substrate, and installation of new siding with proper flashing and trim details.

Siding Repair

Storm damage, impact damage, or isolated sections of deteriorated siding don't always require full replacement. Our repair services address localized problems while blending seamlessly with existing siding. We keep common siding profiles in stock and can often match discontinued products to maintain a consistent appearance.

Siding Installation for New Construction

Building a new home or addition? We work with builders of new homes and homeowners managing their own builds to provide quality siding installation on new construction projects. Proper installation on new construction includes correct weather barrier installation, proper flashing integration with windows and doors, and appropriate clearance from roofing and landscaping.

Trim and Accent Work

Beautiful siding installation includes attention to trim details that frame windows, doors, corners, and fascia boards. We install coordinating trim in materials that match or complement your siding choice. Many homeowners choose to mix materials—for example, vinyl siding with fiber cement trim for a premium look at moderate cost.

Signs Your Siding Needs Attention

  • Warped or buckled panels: Heat damage or improper installation causing visible distortion
  • Cracks or holes: Entry points for moisture and pests
  • Fading or peeling paint: Indicates UV damage and lost protective finish
  • Rot or soft spots: Wood siding showing moisture damage
  • Loose or missing sections: Storm damage or failed fasteners
  • Increased energy bills: Poor siding insulation affecting efficiency
  • Mold or mildew growth: Moisture trapped behind siding
  • Interior wall damage: Water intrusion through failing siding

Why Replace Siding and Roofing Together?

Many homeowners choose to replace siding during a roofing project, and for good reason. When both projects are done together, you benefit from single-project pricing and scheduling, proper integration of flashing where roof meets walls, consistent color coordination of all exterior materials, one point of contact and single warranty coverage, and reduced overall disruption to your daily life.

If your roof is approaching replacement age and your siding shows signs of wear, bundling both projects often makes the most financial sense. We provide combined estimates so you can compare the costs and benefits of tackling both at once versus separate projects.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does new siding cost in Raleigh?

Siding costs vary by material, home size, and complexity. Vinyl siding typically costs $4-8 per square foot installed, while fiber cement ranges from $8-14 per square foot. A typical 2,000 square foot home might cost $8,000-$16,000 for vinyl or $16,000-$28,000 for fiber cement including labor. Contact us for a free estimate based on your specific home.

How long does siding installation take?

Most residential siding projects take 1-2 weeks depending on home size and complexity. Factors affecting timeline include weather conditions, amount of trim work, and whether underlying repairs are needed. We provide a projected timeline with your estimate.

Which siding lasts longest?

Fiber cement and engineered wood siding typically last 50+ years with proper maintenance. Quality vinyl lasts 30-40 years. Natural wood can last 20-40 years depending on maintenance and climate exposure. We help you choose based on your priorities for longevity, appearance, and budget.

Is fiber cement worth the extra cost?

For many homeowners, yes. Fiber cement offers superior durability, fire resistance, and a more authentic wood-like appearance than vinyl. The longer lifespan and reduced maintenance often offset the higher upfront cost over time. It's especially popular on higher-end homes and in neighborhoods with strict aesthetic standards.

Can you match my existing siding for repairs?

In most cases, yes. We maintain inventory of common siding profiles and can source matching materials for repairs. For discontinued products, we work to find the closest match available. If matching isn't possible, strategic placement can minimize visibility of slight color variations.
